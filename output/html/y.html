yet/jEt/

1a adverb If something hasn't happened yet, it hasn't happened up to now, but it may do so in future. 'What did he say?' 'I haven't asked him yet.'

b adverb If something shouldn't be done yet, it is too early to do it now. It should be done later. Don't take the cake out of the oven yetwait another five minutes or so.

2 adverb You can use yet with comparative forms to emphasise them. Roger is taller than Philip, but Brendan is taller yet. Prices are likely to rise yet higher. The decision has caused yet more controversy.

3 conjunction In rather formal language, yet can be used to point out a contrast. He has been a very bad father, yet his children still love him.
